“Vast amounts (circa ten trillion US dollars) are being expended on scientific research across the world and a very significant number of scientific researchers devote themselves to it.” -R. Dan, G. Paul


Data is a collection of raw facts, figures, numbers, words, or observations that provide information about something, forming the building blocks for understanding and decision-making

Research encompasses our daily lives. From every waking hour, we gather data from our surroundings and use the data as information to gain further understanding into the truth. As long as the information is correct and factual, we have already gathered data.

There are different types of data gathered:

  • Quantitative Data:
    • Measurable and expressed numerically, such as time, distance, or sales figures
  • Qualitative Data
    • Descriptive, non-numerical data that defines characteristics or categories, like colors, customer feedback, or opinions
  • Primary Data
    • Data collected directly by researchers for a specific purpose, often through experiments or surveys.
